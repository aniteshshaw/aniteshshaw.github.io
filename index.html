Case Studies

Where the impact shows up

Three problems I owned end to end — from what was broken to what changed because of it.

Agentic AI for Quality Engineering 60–70% less analysis/design effort

QIS — QA Intelligence System

Senior QA judgment — story analysis, risk modeling, test design, PR validation — lived in a handful of people's heads and didn't scale across a 30+ engineer org. I built QIS, an MCP-powered agentic AI workflow on GitHub Copilot extensibility: custom agents, reusable AI skills, and spec kits that turn that judgment into a repeatable, reviewable process. It cut QA analysis and design effort 60–70%, was adopted across Dev and QE squads, and won an enterprise Technology Innovation Award and an internal AI hackathon.

Release & Delivery Transformation 6 wks → 2 wks release cycle

Shrinking the release cycle without shrinking quality

Releases ran on a 6-week cycle, slowed further by false-positive defects clogging the readiness signal. I redesigned the automation architecture and shift-left governance model and built the iADD service-readiness dashboard to surface real risk early. Release cycles dropped to 2 weeks, iADD cut false defects 70–75% and saves 4–6 hours per incident, and the org has sustained ~98% customer satisfaction with zero escalations.

AI Engineering Tools 25 min → 2–3 min setup

AIssueBotExpress & LM Quick Installer

Two manual bottlenecks slowing teams down: defect triage and environment setup. I built AIssueBotExpress, an AI-powered triage agent that auto-summarizes issues and surfaces probable root causes — it won an internal AI hackathon. Alongside it, LM Quick Installer automated environment provisioning, cutting setup time from 25 minutes to 2–3 minutes.
